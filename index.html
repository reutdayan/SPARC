Abstract

Graphs play a central role in modeling complex relationships in data, yet most graph learning methods falter when faced with cold-start nodes—new nodes lacking initial connections—due to their reliance on adjacency information. To tackle this, we propose SPARC, a groundbreaking framework that introduces a novel approach to graph learning by utilizing generalizable spectral embeddings. With a simple yet powerful enhancement, SPARC empowers state-of-the-art methods to make predictions on cold-start nodes effectively. By eliminating the need for adjacency information during inference and effectively capturing the graph’s structure, we make these methods suitable for real-world scenarios where new nodes frequently appear. Experimental results demonstrate that our framework outperforms existing models on cold-start nodes across tasks such as node classification, node clustering, and link prediction. SPARC provides a solution to the cold-start problem, advancing the field of graph learning.

Main Contributions

SPARC framework: enables prediction on cold-start nodes without adjacency.

Generalizable spectral embeddings: learned from graph structure, applied to unseen nodes.

Unified integration: works with GCNs, Transformers, and Mamba-based models.

Broad impact: improves classification, clustering, link prediction, and scalability.

Strong results: outperforms leading baselines on multiple graph benchmarks.

Motivation

Most graph learning methods rely on adjacency information to understand the structure of a graph. However, in real-world settings — like social networks or recommendation systems — new nodes often appear without any known connections, creating the cold-start problem. Traditional GNNs fail to infer on such nodes, as they cannot access neighborhood information.

SPARC addresses this challenge by learning a generalizable spectral embedding that captures the graph’s structural properties during training and applies them at inference — without using adjacency. This allows models to map unseen nodes into the spectral space and infer their relationships effectively.

Results

SPARC achieves state-of-the-art performance across major graph learning tasks — classification, clustering, and link prediction — maintaining high accuracy even for cold-start nodes without adjacency information.
